## Art. 2 - Purpose of the call

1. As part of the annual plan of action to implement the Right to University Education of the Veneto Region Veneto for the 2025/2026 academic year and in compliance with the National Recovery and Resilience Plan (NRRP) which allocates further financial resources, funded by both the European Union - Next Generation EU and the mission 4, component 1, investment 1.7 “Scholarships for University access” of the NRRP, the Ca’ Foscari University of Venice issues the call for the assignment of scholarships for capable and deserving students in difficult financial conditions. 

2. The aim of the Scholarship is to contribute to covering the maintenance costs incurred by students over at least ten months for each year of the course. The NRRP financial resources are aimed at increasing the number and the amount of the scholarships for capable and deserving students in difficult financial conditions.

The first year of enrollment is defined as the academic year in which a student enrolls for the first time at any university. ## Art. 5 - Definition of student status

1. For the purpose of accessing benefits, student status is defined—according to the classification "local / commuter / non-resident student" described below—based on the distance between the student’s residence and the location of the degree program attended. The distance is calculated between the train station of the student’s town of residence (or the bus station, particularly if no train station exists in the town of residence) and the train or bus station in the town where the course is held, using the fastest available public transportation. A student is classified as follows: 

a) local, students residing in the municipality or in the area surrounding the location of the study programme attended (within $4 0 \ \mathsf { k m }$ ). A student is also classified as local if they are enrolled in a study program that is conducted entirely or predominantly online, regardless of their place of residence in relation to the administrative location of the enrolled degree program. 

b) commuter, students residing in a place that allows daily transfer to the location of the study programme attended within a distance of over $4 0 \ \mathsf { k m }$ and within 80 km and/or travel time of over 40 km and within 80 minutes; students resident in the municipality where the study programme is based may also be considered commuters, in relation to particular distances or travel times on public transport. The decision will be taken in accordance with ESU Venezia; 

c) non-resident, students residing in a place far from the location of the study programme attended (over 80 km or over 80 minutes away) and who, for this reason, pay for accommodation near that location (i.e. in the municipality where the university is located or in a municipality classified as local), using public residential facilities or other private or corporate housing for a period of at least ten months (or five months in case of students applying for the scholarship exclusively for the last semester) with reference to the academic year 2025/2026. If a student resides in a place far from the course location but lives in accommodation near the course for free, they are considered a commuter student. 

Students residing in a place far from the location of the attended study course who pay for accommodation near the course for a period of less than ten months (or less than five months for students who apply for the scholarship for the last semester), but who, in the same academic year, attend a study programme abroad (including work experience and internships, provided they are mandatory according to one's curriculum) promoted by the university, or who carry out an internship in Italy, regularly inserted in their exam booklet, or who attend the “Italian Erasmus” programme, in a place over 80 km or 80 minutes from their course of studies and the place of their residence, are in any case assigned the status of non-resident. In order to confirm their status, they are required to pay for accommodation abroad (in the place of their study programme) or in the Italian city where they carry out the internship or where the partner University of the “Italian Erasmus” programme is located, for a period that, combined with the period in Italy, is at least ten months (or at least five months for those who apply for the scholarship for the last semester). Students must present a copy of the rental contract translated into Italian or English, according to the procedures and timing indicated in paragraph 2 below. 

For internships that are mandatory according to students’ curriculum, the rental contract must be referred to the city where they carry out the internship. Rental contracts that last longer than the duration of the internship (as indicated in the training project) will not be accepted. 

Students enrolled in PhD courses shall also be considered non-resident regardless of the place of residence and shall not be required to present any certification of election of domicile; in this case, the maximum amount of €540,00 for catering services shall be withheld from the scholarship. 

Non-EU foreign students, whose household is not resident in Italy, are considered non-resident, regardless of the distance between the residence and the location of the study course. However, to confirm nonresident status, students must pay for accommodation in the municipality of the university attended or in a municipality classified as local, for a period of at least ten months (five months for those who apply for the scholarship for the last semester), with reference to the academic year 2025/2026, (therefore between September 2025 and September 2026) and they must meet the requirements outlined in paragraph 2 below. Students who do not meet the requirements will be considered commuter students.

The definition of “local, commuter and non-resident” student status, is made with reference to the tables available at universities and ESUs (Agencies for the Right to University Education), which can be consulted in Italian on the website www.unive.it/dirittoallostudio $>$ Borse per il diritto allo studio, unless proven otherwise as provided by the student through the presentation of official documents issued by the transport service providers. 

If the student’s residence is included in the list of commuter or local municipalities in relation to the course venue, they cannot be considered non-resident, even if they present a legitimate rental contract.

The reference average is given by the average of the grades achieved by the students in the same exams and in the same academic year as the applicant student. The merit index (I) is given by the difference between the number of credits held by the student (CS) and the minimum number of credits required for access (CR), to which is added the difference between the average grade of the student (MS) and the reference average grade (MR) multiplied by a different weighting coefficient $( P )$ for each degree programme, inversely proportional to the difference between 30 and the degree programme average. ## Art. 16 - Declarations: corrections and verifications

1. Benefit applications, accompanied by information on financial situation and merit, are presented by students with the right of self-certification pursuant to Presidential Decree no. 445 of 28 December 2000. 

2. The University is obliged to check the truthfulness of self-certification presented by students by means of spot checks, which involve at least $20 \%$ of those eligible to benefit from special services and measures each year. 

3. For the purpose of the checks on the self-certifications and documents presented by students (ex. rental contract for confirming the non-resident student status) the University may request documentary evidence of the 

completeness and truthfulness of the information declared, including for the purpose of correcting material or minor errors. 

4. The controls and checks on the truthfulness of student self-certification can also be performed after the benefit has been paid and with the help of the Italian finance police, to which the University sends the declarations for the relevant inspections. Direct checks may be carried out to verify the truthfulness of the information provided, performed by the Italian finance police with the banks and other financial intermediaries that manage personal assets, pursuant to Art.11, paragraph 6 of Prime Ministerial decree no. 159 of 5 December 2013; the university may also check the truthfulness of the declared household situation and perform comparisons of data on income and assets. 

5. Should due administrative and tax checks reveal a declaration to be untrue, any benefit awarded shall be forfeited, even in the case of material or minor errors. This is without prejudice to the provisions of the national legislation in force. In particular, we refer to Articles 71 paragraph 1 bis, 75 and 76 of Presidential Decree no. 445/2000, the Criminal Code and Article 10, paragraph 3 of Legislative Decree no. 68/2012 which reads: “Whoever [omissis] submits untruthful declarations [omissis] shall be subject to an administrative sanction consisting of the payment of a sum of three times the amount received, or the value of the services unduly used, and shall lose the right to receive other benefits for the duration of the study programme, without prejudice in any case to the application of the sanctions referred to in Article 38, paragraph 3 of Decree-Law no. 78 of 31 May 2010, converted, with amendments, by Law no. 122 of 30 July 2010, and in the penal legislation for criminal acts." 

6. Students must notify the Financial Aid Office (dirittoallostudio@unive.it) promptly of any event occurring after the date of the submission of the application that is significant for the purpose of awarding the scholarship, of the status (non-resident, commuter, local) or of revoking the scholarship,
