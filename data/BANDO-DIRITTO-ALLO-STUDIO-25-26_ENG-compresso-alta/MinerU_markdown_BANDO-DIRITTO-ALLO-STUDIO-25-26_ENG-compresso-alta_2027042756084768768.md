# SECTION I – GENERAL PRINCIPLES AND DEFINITIONS

# Art.1 General principles

The Lazio Region, through DiSCo, the regional body for the right to education and the promotion of knowledge, protects and promotes the right to university education as a fundamental tool for “removing the economic and social barriers that limit the full development of the human person” (Article 3 of the Constitution of the Italian Republic). 

Through this Call for applications, DiSCo regulates access to the benefits for the academic year 2025/2026. 

Benefits may be requested yearly, until the highest level of university education is attained. Benefits cannot be requested while the student is obtaining a second degree of the same cycle as the one already achieved(e.g. a double three-year degree). 

Benefits are awarded by calculating and combining elements such as the applicant’s income and merit. 

Specific categories of students may benefit from facilitations and exemptions, such as students with disability of no less than $66 \%$ .

Thanks to this Call for applications and subject to the available funds, DiSCo grants the following: 

• Scholarships for degree programmes under Art. 4; 

• Accommodation places at DiSCo’s university Residences, under Annex G ; 

• Subsidised accommodation places

Graduation awards for the beneficiaries of a scholarship in the academic year 2024/2025 who complete, in 2024/2025, a first-year or second-year degree or a Master’s degree within the legal duration of the relevant course of study. 

# Art.4 Beneficiaries

Students who are enrolled as at 10 February 2026 in one of the following courses at a University with headquarters in the Lazio Region are permitted access to the benefits: 

• Degree; 

• Master’s degree; 

• Single-cycle Master’s degree; 

• PhD programmes, except for students benefitting from scholarships, research grants, or any other grants from the University or other Institutions; 

• Post-graduate school, except for medical students who are the beneficiaries of scholarships, internship contracts or other benefits and remuneration; 

• Joint study courses, on condition that the students are enrolled at a university with legal headquarters in the Lazio Region and have paid the regional tax for the right to study in the Lazio Region. 

Students are entitled to scholarships, if they meet the income- and merit-based requirements, have been declared beneficiaries following the publication of the relative rankings, and their universities have confirmed the following even after the publication of the final results: 

• Their regular enrolment; 

• Their regular university degree; 

• The consistency between the data contained in the student’s application and the findings. 

The benefits referred to in this call for applications are granted for the first-time completion of each of the above course levels. 

The following student categories are not admitted to this call and are therefore considered excluded: 

• Students with another university qualification, even if obtained abroad, equal to the title they intend to obtain at the end of the course they are enrolled in for the academic year 2025/2026; 

• Students who, after changing degrees, have already obtained a scholarship for the same type of course for which they applied under this call for applications; 

Students who have not participated directly in this call for applications and who submit an application to participate in the benefits for academic year 2025/2026, asking to transfer their ranking position from other Institutions for the right to study. 

Students admitted to Specialisation Schools with deferred enrolment, governed by calls for applications issued by their respective universities, may access the benefits hereunder only if, as at 10 February 2026, the year of enrolment coincides with the academic year 2025/2026. 

# Double enrolment

Students for the 2025/2026 academic year who have enrolled in two courses at the same time1 may benefit from the conditions and services promoting the right to study only for one of the two courses taken at a University with legal headquarters in the Lazio Region. Such enrolment must be indicated in the online application. For the second course, a higher scholarship amount is envisaged, as per Art. 8 of the call for applications. 

A student with one or more careers already started may apply for a scholarship, indicating the earliest enrolment date or, in case of enrolment to courses of different levels, the enrolment date of the higher course of studies. 

Students must maintain the merit-based requirements for both courses of study to be entitled to the higher amount for the double enrolment. 

# Students enrolled in the Faculty of Medicine in academic year 2025/2026

Pending issuance of the implementation decrees referred to in Delegated Law no. 26 of 14 March 2025, students enrolled in the Faculty of Medicine at the Universities of the Lazio Region in academic year 2025/2026 and subject to the so-called “filter semester” may apply for the benefits referred to in this call for applications, also for the purpose of taking a census of such students and to facilitate and accelerate future operations relating to the benefits to be paid. In any case, DiSCo, at the end of the filter semester and only for students who have been enrolled in it, will proceed to reopen the application form for benefits for academic year 2025/2026. 

If the teaching activities of the filter semester are carried out mainly in person $15 \%$ of teaching), without prejudice to DiSCo’s inability to disburse the scholarship during this period, those students who have submitted the application for benefits may be assigned the canteen services and accommodation at a subsidised rate. 

The above is without prejudice to DiSCo’s duty to govern the case pursuant to the previous paragraph differently, based on the issuance of any implementation decrees of Delegated Law no. 26 of 14 March 2025, if they contain different provisions with respect to what is envisaged in this call for applications. 

# Art.5 Compatibility/incompatibility

# Compatibility

The DiSCo scholarship is exclusively compatible with: 

• internship grants (150 hours); 

• grants issued by the European Union and/or other entities with different objectives; 

• other grants that can be accumulated as specified by the law (e.g. Erasmus grants). 

# Scholarships that are irrelevant for the submission of the application form

For the purposes of this call for applications, international mobility grants and 150-hour grants need not be declared when the application is submitted. 

Scholarships and grants awarded for academic years other than the academic year covered by this notice are also irrelevant. 

Incompatibility Scholarships granted by DiSCo are incompatible with scholarships and/or other benefits for the right to study awarded by other public and private bodies/entities, including those assigned by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and merit-based scholarships allocated by universities (e.g.: Wanted the Best, Don’t Miss Your Chance, Technopole, etc.). 

The scholarships granted by DiSCo are incompatible with scholarships, research grants or other benefits awarded to PhD students by the university or other Institutions. 

In order for the student awarded another scholarship/benefit for academic year 2025/2026 to maintain the benefits provided by DiSCo, they must submit, in the section “Available call operations” of their personal account and within 15 days from the appointment, the documentation relating to the incompatible benefit issued by the other institution, as well as the documentation certifying the repayment of any amounts already received. 

# Art.6 Duration of benefits

With regard to the type of course of studies, the benefits may be required from the year of first enrolment: 

Throughout the legal duration of the courses plus a semester in case of enrolment in a first- or secondyear degree, or single-cycle Master’s degree, including music and art degrees AFAM under the post-1999/”new” system; plus an additional semester only for the accommodation service; 

Throughout the legal duration of the courses, in case of enrolment in a PhD. Beneficiaries of scholarships under MD n. 224/1999 or of other contributions from universities or other public or private institutions are excluded; 

Throughout the legal duration of the courses, in case of enrolment in a PhD. Beneficiaries of scholarships under MD n. 368/1999 or of other contributions from universities or other public or private institutions are excluded. 

This call for applications contains the following exemptions: 

a) Students with disability equal to or greater than $66 \%$ as per Art. 2, letter f, benefit from two additional semesters in terms of duration of the benefit, as set forth for the cases contemplated in this call for applications. In addition, such students are awarded a $10 \%$ increase of the scholarship amount compared to other students. 

b) Students with a three-year degree who enrol in a single-cycle degree course. 

Students with a first-cycle three-year or bachelor’s degree who enrol in a single- cycle degree are eligible for these benefits only starting from the 4th year and for the remaining years admissible to complete the single-cycle degree. 

The year of first enrolment should be calculated from the first year of enrolment, minus the legal duration of the three-year degree obtained. 

c) Students with a degree obtained abroad. Students in possession of a foreign university degree, even if it relates to a university based in Italy, are required, under penalty of forfeiture of benefits, to declare their possession in the application form and may only take advantage of benefits for the right to study for the level of study subsequent to that recognised in Italy for the foreign degree already obtained. 

DiSCo reserves the right to request, even after the publication of the final results, the official documentation (Academic Recognition, CIMEA certificate or ARDI certificate, DOV etc.) attesting the level of studies in Italy corresponding to the academic qualification obtained abroad. In such cases, DiSCo will place a freeze on the competition position and the student must produce, under penalty of exclusion from benefits, the required documentation by the deadline and according to the procedures communicated by the Administration. The deadline of 10 February 2026 for regularising the competition position remains unchanged. 

This is without prejudice to DiSCo’s right to acquire relevant documentation ex officio. 

Without prejudice to letter b of this article, in the event of degree shortening, which is requested from the university for the purpose of obtaining the degree, students will only be able to take advantage of the benefits for the right to study for the remaining years of the course required to obtain the degree.

# Art.15 Criteria for the definition of rankings

Students are placed in the rankings based on the data provided on their application forms, after verification of any false statements with the competent authorities. 

After verifying that the students provided correct declarations, meet the requirements set forth by this call for applications, the ranking position and availability of funds for each benefit, students will be classified as follows 

• “Beneficiary”; 

• “Eligible”; 

• “Excluded”. 

Benefits are only granted to students declared “beneficiaries”. 

Students who, despite meeting the requirements, are not declared beneficiaries due to a lack of economic resources are declared eligible. 

The selection of the students8 beneficiary of the grants referred to in this call for applications is made according to the following criteria: 

a)	 First-year students: DiSCo, in accordance with the principles of equal treatment and to ensure balanced access to the scholarship by both EU and non-EU students, draws up the rankings as follows: 

- for students belonging to the EU and enrolled in their 1st year, the provisional and final rankings are drawn up in ascending order of income based on the ISEE9. When the ISEE indicator is equal, priority is given to older students; 

- 	for non-EU students enrolled in their 1st year, the provisional and final rankings are drawn up in ascending order of income based on the ISEEUP10. When the ISEEUP indicator is equal, priority is given to older students; 

DiSCo allocates the resources to students enrolled in their 1st year in proportion to the number of regular applications submitted by EU and non-EU students and grants the scholarships based on the position of students in the relative ranking. 

b)	 Post first-year students: provisional and final rankings are based on a “joint coefficient”, calculated with the following formula: 

$$
C = 0. 7 \times C F N + 0. 0 4 \times M e a n N + 0. 2 6 \times I S E E N
$$

Where CFN is the normalised merit coefficient adjusted to the use of the bonus, if any, and a potential higher number of credits than those laid down by the Prime Ministerial Decree: 

$$
\mathrm {C F N} = \frac {\left(\text {A c h i e v e d m e r i t - M i n i m u m e x p e c t e d m e r i t}\right)}{\left(\text {M a x i m u m a c h i e v a b l e m e r i t - M i n i m u m e x p e c t e d m e r i t}\right)}
$$

The MeanN is the normalised mean of the grades: 

$$
\text {M e a n N} = \frac {\left(\text {M e a n - 1 8}\right)}{(3 0 - 1 8)}
$$

and ISEEN is the normalised ISEE indicator: 

$$
\text {I S S E N} = 1 - \frac {\left(\text {I S E E D S U}\right)}{\left(\text {I S E E - m a x}\right)}
$$

Where max is the maximum ISEE/ISEEUP threshold set out in the call for applications. 

c)	 Subsequent years of PhD or specialisation programmes: rankings are strictly in ascending order of ISEE. 

Every territorial office will provide the beneficiaries’ lists to the Universities of reference for any subsequent procedure. As regards the assignment of the beneficiary status, the documentation acquired by 10 February 2026 will be considered.

# SPECIAL CASES

# Bonus for students coming from universities in other regions

For students who have transferred to a Lazio-based University from a non-Lazio-based University, the bonus requested at the time the application was submitted will only be awarded once DiSCo has verified the residual bonus credits with the University of origin. 

# Students excluded from the bonus

The following students cannot use the bonus: 

a)	 Students who have obtained the recognition of credits/exams passed abroad, including the credits for foreign academic qualifications; 

b)	 Post first-year students who have already earned enough credits to obtain the benefits specified in this call for applications; 

c)	 Students who have obtained a shortening of the degree programme, for any reason; 

d)	 Students already having a degree obtained abroad; 

e)	 Students enrolled in a second-cycle specialization degree with an entry degree from a pre-1999 (old system) programme; 

f)	 First-year students of a first-cycle three-year degree, second-cycle specialization degree, or singlecycle specialization or master’s degree; 

g)	 Students enrolled in a second-cycle specialization degree, who have moved from a pre-1999 (old system with exam-based merit) to a post-1999 (new system) course to obtain their first-cycle threeyear degree.

# SECTION XI – FINAL PROVISIONS

# Art.47 Checks, revocations, penalties, disclosure requirements

According to the applicable regulations, DiSCo thoroughly checks if the declarations produced by beneficiary or eligible students are true and correct. 

To this purpose, other than asking the students for any record that can substantiate the truthfulness of their statements, DiSCo can conduct any investigations it may deem reasonable, even with the help of the competent authorities, public bodies or law enforcement agencies, including: 

a)	 Tax police (Polizia Tributaria); 

b)	 Guardia di Finanza; 

c)	 State Police; 

d)	 Municipalities; 

e)	 Land and territorial agency (Agenzia del Territorio); 

f)	 Schools of all levels; g) Mutual entities; 

h)	 INPS (National Social Security Institute); 

i)	 Universities; 

j)	 Italian Tax Authority (Agenzia delle Entrate); 

k)	 Embassies or Consulates; 

l)	 Other bodies and organizations that provide services or benefits for the Right to Education; 

m)	 Other offices that hold data relative to economic conditions/merit and personal status. 

# 47.1 Assessment of inconsistencies and related sanction

In the event that, during the course of verification procedures, discrepancies are found between the students’ statements and the findings of the inspections, DiSCo will proceed to revoke the benefits and initiate the applicable administrative procedure against the student concerned. 

If the investigations prove that the students made false statements about themselves or their families, without prejudice to Art. 316 ter of the Criminal Code, such students will lose all benefits and will be fined to pay three times the amount received and to refund the cost of all services used, and they will no longer be entitled to receive any grant whatsoever throughout their academic career, as specified in Art. 10, paragraph 3 of Legislative Decree no. 68/2012. 

# Art.48 Termination rule for cases not contemplated in this notice

For the cases not covered in the previous articles, DiSCo reserves the right to apply the measures regulating similar cases or matters, the general principles of the rules, and the practices and customs consolidated by the Administration, in this order. 

However, DiSCo’s technical-administrative discretion will be exercised. 

In the cases described in the above paragraph, the General Director, upon proposal of the Manager in charge of scholarships and benefits, shall adopt the relevant measure. 

# Art.49 Confidentiality and Privacy

Prior to the completion of the online application, a specific notice will inform students that DiSCo will process their data and information, pursuant to Art. 13 GDPR 679/2016. 

Personal data are processed only for the exclusive purpose of giving due execution to all the obligations under this call for applications. 

In any case, DiSCo undertakes to process the personal data provided according to the principles of correctness, lawfulness and transparency in full compliance with the aforementioned European Regulation No. 679/2016 and Legislative Decree no. 196/2003 and subsequent amendments and additions, with particular regard to the safety measures to be taken, pursuant to Art. 32 of EU Regulation 679/2016. In particular, in the implementation of all the activities related to the execution of the requirements of this call and that may involve the processing of personal data, DiSCo undertakes to act in accordance with the regulations in force by observing appropriate organizational and technical measures, as well as suitable actions to ensure the security of information in terms of confidentiality, availability and integrity of the personal data processed and to prevent the risk of destruction, loss or alteration, even accidental, of data and information. 

For the purposes of this call for applications and any connected activities, the Data Protection Officer is the General Director, Via Cesare De Lollis 24b, 00185 Rome.
