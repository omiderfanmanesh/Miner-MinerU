# Article 1 – Benefits of the Competition Notice

1. The benefits this competition is held for are the following ones: 

a) scholarship: the payment of a monetary amount that varies according to the University ISEE and the type of student “On-site”, “Commuting”, “Off-site”; 

b) additional amounts to the scholarship: payment of an additional amount to the scholarship monetary amount for students meeting the requirements referred to in article 12 of this Notice; 

c) the accommodation: for “Off-site” students, the free use of accommodation in the university residences with any supplements paid by the student. 

The scholarship, the related supplements and the accommodation are benefits attributed annually, on request, to students meeting the requirements specified in this Notice. The application must be submitted online annually within the terms established by this Notice. 

2. Scholarship winners are also entitled to: 

a) to the daily use of a first meal free of charge and a second meal at a reduced rate at the catering centres of ALiSEO; 

b) to the exemption from the payment of contributions due to the University of Genoa or to the Institutions for Higher Education in Art and Music (AFAM) and of the regional tax for the University Right to Study. 

# Article 2 – Eligible students

1. Students who are eligible for the benefits under this notice, limited to the achievement of the first degree for each level of study, are those enrolled for a.y. 2025/2026 in: 

a) the following full-time courses run by the University of Genoa (hereafter “University”): 

first level bachelor degree courses, single-cycle master's degree courses, second level master's degree courses; 

specialisation courses, at least two years in duration, with an exception for the medical area according to Italian Legislative Decree no. 368 of 17 August 1999; PhD courses activated according to Italian Legislative Decree no. 210 of 3 July 1998, Art. 4who do not benefit from the university scholarship; 

b) study courses run by the Institutions for Higher Education in Art and Music (AFAM), based in Liguria, pursuant to Table B attached to this notice: 

 first-level specialist degree; 

second-level specialist degree (or equivalent); 

previous system degree. 

2. The title obtained for the completion of studies at Fine Arts Academies, State Schools of Music and equivalent musical Institutes (Current System) and at the end of university diploma courses, is conventionally considered as equivalent to the bachelor degree (first level title) and, as such, enables the student to compete for the concession of a scholarship and accommodation only for a master's degree, or a single-cycle master's degree, as long as, in this latter case, admission has taken place or takes place during years after the first in accordance with Article 3.5 paragraph 1. 

3. The previous system degree (hereinafter also referred to as the “PS degree”) is conventionally considered equivalent to a master's degree (second level title) and allows the student to compete for the scholarship and accommodation but only in order to achieve the specialisation or PhD courses. 

4. The diploma obtained after completion of previous system studies, completed at State Schools of Music and equivalent Musical Institutes, entitles the student to compete for a scholarship for any three-year course, as long as it is not run by the Institutes in question. 

# 3.1 Scholarship

1. Students may obtain a scholarship for the first title, only for each level of study, for a maximum time, calculated from the year of first enrolment, of: 

a) if enrolled in first-level bachelor degree courses: a total of 7 semesters (6 semesters plus an additional semester); 

b) if enrolled in single-cycle master's degree courses: a total of 11 semesters for students enrolled in courses with a legal duration of 5 years, a total of 13 semesters for students enrolled in courses with a legal duration of 6 years (a period corresponding to the duration provided by the respective educational systems plus an additional semester); 

c) if enrolled in second-level master's degree courses: a total of 5 semesters (4 semesters plus an additional semester); 

d) if enrolled in AFAM Institutions: period corresponding to the legal duration of the courses; 

e) if enrolled in PhD courses or specialisation courses: duration established by the respective teaching systems. 

# 3.2. Accommodation

1. The duration of the free concession of accommodation limited to the first degree for each level of study, for a maximum period of time which is calculated from the year of first enrolment, is equal to: 

a) if enrolled in first-level bachelor degree courses: a total of 8 semesters (6 semesters, plus 2 semesters); 

b) if enrolled in single-cycle master’s degree courses: a total of 12 semesters for students enrolled in courses with a legal duration of 5 years, a total of 14 semesters for students enrolled in courses with a legal duration of 6 years (duration established by the respective teaching systems plus 2 semesters); 

c) if enrolled in second-level master's degree courses: a total of 6 semesters (4 semesters plus 2 semesters); 

d) if enrolled in the Institutions for High Artistic, Musical and Performing Arts Education: duration established by the legal duration of the courses; 

e) if enrolled in for PhD courses or specialisation courses: a period corresponding to the duration established by the respective didactic regulations. 

2. For students referred to in letters a), b), c) of section 3.1 paragraph 1, the second additional semester is granted against payment of €200,00 per month if in ALiSEO Residences. 

# 3.3. Scholarship for disabled students

1. Students with disabilities referred to in Article 13 of this Competition Notice, can obtain the scholarship, limitated to for the first title for each level of study, for a maximum period of time calculated from the year of first enrolment as follows: 

a) if enrolled in first-level bachelor degree courses: a total of 9 semesters (6 semesters plus 3 additional semesters); 

b) if enrolled in single-cycle master’s degree courses: a total of 13 semesters for students enrolled in courses with a legal duration of 5 years, a total of 15 semesters for students enrolled in courses with a legal duration of 6 years (duration established by the respective teaching systems plus 3 semesters); 

c) if enrolled in second-level master's degree courses: a total of 7 semesters (4 semesters plus 3 semesters); 

d) if enrolled in the Institutions for Higher Education in Art and Music (AFAM): duration established by the legal duration of the courses; 

e) if enrolled in for PhD courses or specialisation courses: duration established by the respective teaching systems. 

# 3.4 Accommodation for disabled students

1. The duration of the free concession of accommodation service, limited to the first degree for each level of study, for a maximum period of time, calculated from the year of first enrolment, is equal to: 

a) if enrolled in first degree courses: a total of 10 semesters (6 semesters plus 4 additional semesters); 

b) if enrolled in single-cycle master’s degree courses: a total of 14 semesters for students enrolled in courses with a legal duration of 5 years, a total of 16 semesters for students enrolled in courses with a legal duration of 6 years (duration established by the respective teaching systems plus 4 semesters); 

c) if enrolled in second-level master's degree courses: a total of 8 semesters (4 semesters plus 4 semesters); 

d) if enrolled in the Institutions for Higher Education in Art and Music (AFAM):: duration established by the legal duration of the courses; 

e) if enrolled in for PhD courses or specialisation courses: duration established by the respective teaching systems. 

2. For students referred to in letters a), b), c) of section 3.3 paragraph 1, the last additional semester is granted against payment of € 200,00 per month if in ALiSEO Residences. 

# 3.5 Clarifications on the duration of the benefits

1. For students who, after attending a bachelor's degree program or a conventionally corresponding (with or without obtaining a degree) are admitted or have been admitted to a single-cycle master's degree program, for the purpose of the duration of the granting of benefits, are counted all years of enrollment from the conventional year of first enrollment in the bachelor's degree, including 2025/2026, and any years of non-enrollment between courses (bachelor's and one-cycle master's degree). 

2. These students may obtain a scholarship and accommodation for a maximum period equal to the difference between the normal duration of the single-cycle master's degree, increased by one semester (two semesters for accommodation – or two semesters for the scholarship and three semesters for the accommodation, in case of students with disabilities referred to in Article 13 of this Competition Notice and the number of years taken to achieve the study title in their possession. 

3. Students who, having achieved a university diploma that allowed them to be admitted to a threeyear degree or a single cycle master's degree, may take part in the selection for a number of semesters equal to the difference between the normal duration of three-year or the single-cycle master's degree, increased by one semester (or three semesters in case of students with disabilities referred to in Article 13 of this Competition Notice and the number of semesters taken to achieve the university diploma. 

4. Students who have obtained the equivalence of a degree achieved abroad cannot apply for the benefits referred to in this Notice for an equivalent level degree, without prejudice to the application of the provisions referred to in the previous paragraphs. 

# Article 4 - Definitions of the type of students

1. The term “on-site student” is used to refer to students residing in the municipality in which the attended course of studies is based. 

2. The term “off-site student” is used to refer to students residing in a municipality that is distant from the place of the attended course and, therefore, request accommodation near the university, using the ALiSEO residential structures and, in case of unavailable free rooms, submit a regular housing contract following the instructions of the art. 22 of the Notice of Competition. Off-site students who request accommodation service must be at least eighteen years of age on the date of the application deadline. 

If students residing in municipalities that are considered as “off-site” with respect to the university locations (pursuant to Table A attached to this notice) should not request accommodation in the residential structures of ALiSEO, they shall be considered as “Commuting” students. 

3. The term “commuting student” is used to refer to students who are not considered as off-site and yet reside in a municipality other than that of the place of the course of studies attended (see Table A attached to this Notice), who do not require accommodation in the ALiSEO residential structures or in residential structures holding agreements with ALiSEO (in accordance with what stated before). 

4. The online application for benefits provides an automatic system for determining the type of student in function of the location of the attended course of study and of the municipality of residence of the student. This information must be declared by the student in the application. 

5. If the "off-site" students renounce the benefit of the accommodation place or are ineligible for the rental contribution (referred to in Article 22 of this Notice), they receive the disbursement of the monetary installment of the scholarship as a "commuter." 

6.Students who believe that they should actually be classified differently, on the basis of the travel time necessary to reach the municipality where the attended course of studies is based using public means of transport, they may request their status to be reviewed at the same time as submitting their application for a scholarship. To this purpose, they must therefore submit a specific, duly documented request to this end, showing the time taken to reach the municipality where the attended course of studies is based, from their place of residence. The Head of the Department Right to Study of ALiSEO evaluates the request at his sole discretion within the date as indicated in the art. 23 paragraph 6 of the Notice. 

7. The international non-EU students residing in Italy and applying for a place in ALiSEO's residential facilities are considered to be “off-site” with the exception of those whose household resides in Italy. In this case, in order to determine the “typology” of the student the actual residence of the family unit is considered,. 

8. The “independent” student, referred to in art. 11.4.1 of this Competition Notice, is considered to be “off-site” regardless of their place of residence, in accordance with the provisions of Ministry of Universities and Research Decree no. 1320 of 17 December 2021. 

# Article 5 - Conventional year of first registration, provisions for changes of course or renunciation of studies

1. The requirements of merit, as referred to in article 8 and 9 of this Notice, that must be met by students always refer to the conventional year of first registration. 

The conventional year of first registration is considered to be: 

a) for students registered on first level bachelor degree courses and on single-cycle master's degree courses and on previous system degree courses, the academic year of first registration to University or AFAM institutions; 

b) for students registered on second level degree courses, the academic year of first registration for the second level degree to University or AFAM institutions. 

2. The conventional year of first registration in the following cases is determined as follows: 

a) for University or AFAM students for whom it has been established “the change in course without validation of previous career”, the conventional year of registration is the first year of enrolment to the previous career; 

b) for students who, after having attended a bachelor degree (with or without graduation) are admitted or have been admitted to a single-cycle master's degree, in the presence of 

validation by the University of credits previously obtained, are counted all years of enrollment from the conventional year of first enrollment in the bachelor's degree, including 2025/2026, and any years of non-enrollment between courses (bachelor's and single-cycle master's degrees); 

c) for students to whom credits achieved in previous careers (Italian and/or abroad) are recognized and validated, even after the submission of the application for benefits, the conventional year of enrolment is the first year of enrolment in the previous career; 

d) for students who obtain exemptions from educational obligations through the validation of credits (previously obtained) not delivered in Italian or foreign university courses, the conventional year of registration is the first year of enrolment at the University of Genoa. In order to assess whether or not the requirement of merit is met, only credits acquired from the University of Genoa will be considered. 

e) for students who make the transfer / leave from another Italian University or AFAM Institution, it will be considered as conventional year the one of first enrolment at the course of origin. In order to be admitted to the competition for ALiSEO benefits, students must be validly registered with the University of Genoa by 31 December 2025; 

f) for students who obtain exemptions from educational obligations through the validation of credits previously obtained during international mobility programmes, the conventional year of registration is the first year of enrolment at the University of Genoa. In order to assess whether or not the requirement of merit is met, only credits acquired form the University of Genoa will be considered. 

g) for students who have renounced their studies in connection with their “previous career”, enrolling ex novo to a course for the first time, the years of registration will not be counted and neither will changes of course prior to academic year 2025/2026; 

h) for students who have obtained from University, pursuant to art. 9, paragraph 4 and 5 of Legislative Decree no. 68 of 29 March 2012, the granting of the exemption from the payment of the fees for the interruption of the studies, the years for which the University ordered the exemption are not counted; 

i) for students who obtain exemption from educational obligations through the recognition of credits obtained in the year of enrolment in “individual courses”, the conventional year of first enrolment is the first year of the course for which the benefits are requested. For the purposes of evaluating the merit requirement for obtaining benefits, credits acquired in the year of enrolment in “individual courses” are not considered. 

3. Students who are enrolled for the academic year 2023/2024 cannot apply for benefits for a course year if they have already applied for benefits for the same year to ALiSEO or to another Italian Institutions for right to University study, even in case of a change of course “with” or “without” exam validation and/or renunciation of studies. 

Please note that the aforementioned provision does not apply to students who: 

a) have not completed their enrolment at the University or at AFAM Institutions for the same course year for which they intend to re-apply for benefits; 

b) have been “excluded” from the final ALiSEO rankings for the same course year for which they intend to re-apply for benefits. 

4. On the basis of the M.I.U.R and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the Interior provisions on the matter of access for foreign students to university courses, for the purpose of issuing an entry visa for Italian territory for reasons of study, non-EU students who make study renunciation must return 

to their country of origin and, in order to enrol in another course at the University, they must request a new entry visa for reasons of study.

# 9.1.1 Bonus credits

• 12 credits bonus for the application of the benefits for a third year student ; 

• 15 credits bonus for the application of the benefits for a subsequent third year student .

a) The bonus value increases only if the bonus is not used, up to a maximum of 15 bonus credits;

c) A bonus fully used by students results in the loss of the right to use other bonus points for the rest of their academic career; 

d) The residual bonus amount can be used in the following years;

# 11.4 Independent family unit

# 11.4.1 Independent student

1. In order to apply for the benefits of this Notice, the student constitutes an independent family unit from that of the original family in the presence of both of the following requirements: 

a) personal residence outside the original family home for at least two years from the date of submission of the declaration of single replacement (DSU), in housing not owned by one of its members; 

b) Presence of an adequate capacity for taxed and assimilated income received in tax years 2023 and 2024, not be less than € 9,000.00 for each year.

# Article 19 - Criteria for the conferral of accommodation

# 19.1 Reserves accommodation places

ALiSEO reserves at its Residences no. 8 places for students enrolled in specialisation courses or PhD's (referred to in article 2 of this Notice); these places are assigned in increasing order of ISEEE/ISEEUP, with priority given to students enrolled in years subsequent to the first. 

# 19.2 Confirmation of subsequent years and first year master degree

1. Students who have been awarded a scholarship and are included in the accommodation ranking as years following the first or “seventh semester $^ +$ first year of the Master's degree”, who have already been assigned accommodation in the rankings of the 2024/2025 competition and who have not renounced to it or who have not been revoked during the 2024/2025 AY, are confirmed in the accommodation place occupied in the 2024/2025 AY (confirmations); 

2. Students who have been awarded a scholarship and are included in the ranking as a first-year master's degree student, who have already been assigned accommodation in the rankings of the 2024/2025 competition and who have not renounced to it or who have not been revoked during the 2024/2025 AY, are confirmed in the accommodation place occupied in the 2024/2025 AY (confirmations), as long as they meet one of the following requirements 

a) have completed their first level degree by 31 August 2025; 

b) be in possession of 135 credits as of 10 August 2025 (without the use of the bonus credits). 

# 19.3 New requests of subsequent years

1. The places made available after the assignments pursuant to 19.2 paragraph, will be assigned to students not housed in residences in academic year 2024/2025 enrolled in years subsequent to the first year of all courses (new requests). 

Rankings will be ordered according to decreasing points obtained from the following formula: 

student credits - min. credits 1000 X max. credits - min. credits

The student in the condition of "Eligible Suspended" may be assigned accommodation when he/she corrects his/her position, within the time limits provided in relation to the type of suspension and within the limits of the availability of accommodation at the time the student corrects his/her position.
